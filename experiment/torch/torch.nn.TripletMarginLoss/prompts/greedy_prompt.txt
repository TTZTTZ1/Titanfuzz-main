`TripletMarginLoss` is a PyTorch loss function that measures relative similarity between samples using triplets: an anchor, a positive example (similar to anchor), and a negative example (dissimilar). It computes the loss as:

\[
L = \max(d(a,p) - d(a,n) + \text{margin}, 0)
\]

where \(d\) is the p-norm distance (default L2). The loss encourages the anchor to be closer to the positive than to the negative by at least the specified margin.

**Key parameters:**
- `margin`: Minimum desired separation (default 1.0).
- `p`: Norm degree for distance (default 2).
- `swap`: Optionally use distance swapping as described in the triplet loss literature.
- `reduction`: How to aggregate loss (`'none'`, `'mean'`, `'sum'`).

**Input:** Three tensors of shape `(N, D)` (or `(D)`), representing anchor, positive, and negative samples.

**Use case:** Commonly used in metric learning, face recognition, and embedding tasks to learn discriminative feature representations.
