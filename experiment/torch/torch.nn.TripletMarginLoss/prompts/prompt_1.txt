`TripletMarginLoss` is a PyTorch loss function that measures relative similarity between samples using triplets: anchor (a), positive (p), and negative (n). It enforces that the distance between the anchor and positive is smaller than the distance between the anchor and negative by at least a specified margin.

**Key Features:**
- **Loss Formula**:  
  \( L(a,p,n) = \max\{d(a_i, p_i) - d(a_i, n_i) + \text{margin}, 0\} \)
- **Distance Metric**:  
  \( d(x_i, y_i) = \|x_i - y_i\|_p \), with optional small epsilon for numerical stability.
- **Parameters**:
  - `margin`: Minimum desired separation (default: 1.0).
  - `p`: Norm degree for distance calculation (default: 2, Euclidean).
  - `eps`: Small constant to prevent division by zero (default: 1e-6).
  - `swap`: If `True`, uses distance swapping as described in the referenced paper (default: `False`).
  - `reduction`: Specifies how to aggregate the loss (`'none'`, `'mean'`, `'sum'`; default: `'mean'`).

**Input/Output**:
- **Input**: Three tensors (anchor, positive, negative) of shape `(N, D)` or `(D)`.
- **Output**: A scalar if reduction is applied, or a tensor of shape `(N)` if `reduction='none'`.

**Example Usage**:
```python
loss_fn = nn.TripletMarginLoss(margin=1.0, p=2)
loss = loss_fn(anchor, positive, negative)
loss.backward()
```
