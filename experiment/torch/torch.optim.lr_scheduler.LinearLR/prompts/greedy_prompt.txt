The `LinearLR` scheduler linearly increases the learning rate from a starting factor to an ending factor over a specified number of iterations.

**Key Parameters:**
- `start_factor`: Initial multiplier for the learning rate (default: 1/3).
- `end_factor`: Final multiplier for the learning rate (default: 1.0).
- `total_iters`: Number of iterations over which the linear change occurs (default: 5).

**Behavior:**
- The learning rate is scaled as:  
  `lr = base_lr × [start_factor + (end_factor - start_factor) × (epoch / total_iters)]`  
  until `epoch >= total_iters`, after which `lr = base_lr × end_factor`.
- Works alongside other learning rate modifications.
- Call `scheduler.step()` after each epoch (typically after `optimizer.step()`).

**Example:**
```python
scheduler = LinearLR(optimizer, start_factor=0.05, total_iters=40)
# LR increases linearly from 0.05×base_lr to 1.0×base_lr over 40 epochs.
```
