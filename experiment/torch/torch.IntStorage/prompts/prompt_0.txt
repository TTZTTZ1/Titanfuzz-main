### Core Concept

- **Storage Sharing**: Tensor views (via `view()`, slicing) share the same underlying storage.
- **Gradient Storage**: Tensors with `requires_grad=True` have separate storages for data and gradients.
- **Special Cases**: Meta/Fake tensors have no actual storage; subclasses may behave unusually.

- Fill storage: `storage.fill_(value)`
- Memory mapping: `UntypedStorage.from_file()`
- Shared memory: `storage.share_memory_()`

### Warnings
- Direct storage manipulation is low-level and risky; prefer high-level tensor operations.
- Legacy typed storages (`FloatStorage`, `IntStorage`, etc.) are deprecated.
- Storage APIs are rarely needed in typical use cases.

### Use Case Guidance
Most applications should operate at the tensor level. Storage-level operations are primarily for advanced scenarios like memory optimization, inter-process communication, or debugging tensor relationships.
