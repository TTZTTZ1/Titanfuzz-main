**PairwiseDistance** computes the distance between two sets of vectors using a specified p-norm.  

**Key parameters:**  
- `p`: Norm degree (default 2, Euclidean distance). Can be negative.  
- `eps`: Small constant added to avoid division by zero for negative `p`.  
- `keepdim`: Whether to retain the vector dimension in output shape.  

**Input shapes:**  
- `x1`, `x2`: `(N, D)` (batch of vectors) or `(D)` (single vector).  
- Both inputs must have the same shape.  

**Output shape:**  
- Without `keepdim`: `(N)` or scalar `()` for single vectors.  
- With `keepdim=True`: `(N, 1)` or `(1)`.  

**Formula:**  
\[
\text{dist}(x, y) = \| x - y + \epsilon \cdot \mathbf{1} \|_p
\]
where \(\mathbf{1}\) is a vector of ones.  

**Example:**  
```python
pdist = nn.PairwiseDistance(p=2)
output = pdist(torch.randn(100, 128), torch.randn(100, 128))  # shape: (100,)
```
