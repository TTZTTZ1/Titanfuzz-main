`torch.hsplit` splits a tensor horizontally (along the second dimension) into multiple tensors, each as a view of the original.

**Key points:**
- For 1D tensors, splits along dimension 0.
- For 2D+ tensors, splits along dimension 1 (columns).
- Accepts either an integer (evenly divides the dimension) or a list/tuple of split indices.
- Based on NumPy's `numpy.hsplit`.

**Example:**
```python
t = torch.arange(16.0).reshape(4,4)
torch.hsplit(t, 2)  # Splits into two 4x2 tensors
```
