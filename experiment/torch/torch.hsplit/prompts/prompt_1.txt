`torch.hsplit` splits a tensor **horizontally** (along dimension 1, or dimension 0 for 1D tensors) into multiple views.  

**Key points:**  
- For 1D tensors, equivalent to `torch.tensor_split(input, indices_or_sections, dim=0)`.  
- For 2D+ tensors, equivalent to `torch.tensor_split(input, indices_or_sections, dim=1)`.  
- If `indices_or_sections` is an integer, it must evenly divide the split dimension.  
- Accepts either an integer (number of equal splits) or a list/tuple of split indices.  
- Returns a list of tensors that are views of the original.  

**Example:**  
```python
t = torch.arange(16.0).reshape(4,4)
torch.hsplit(t, 2)  # Splits into two (4,2) tensors
```
