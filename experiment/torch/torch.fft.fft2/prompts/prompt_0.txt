`torch.fft.fft2` computes the 2D discrete Fourier transform of a tensor, applying the FFT to the last two dimensions by default. It is equivalent to `fftn` but limited to two dimensions.

**Key features:**
- Supports real or complex input; for real signals, the output satisfies Hermitian symmetry (`X[i, j] = conj(X[-i, -j])`).
- Use `rfft2` for real inputs to get a compact one-sided output.
- Allows optional zero-padding or trimming via the `s` parameter.
- Normalization modes: `"backward"` (none), `"forward"` (scale by `1/n`), or `"ortho"` (scale by `1/sqrt(n)`).
- On CUDA with SM53+, supports `torch.half` and `torch.chalf` but requires power-of-two lengths in transformed dimensions.

**Example:**
```python
x = torch.rand(10, 10, dtype=torch.complex64)
fft2_result = torch.fft.fft2(x)
```
