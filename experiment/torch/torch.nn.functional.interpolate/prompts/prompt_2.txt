`torch.nn.functional.interpolate` resizes an input tensor to a specified size or scale factor using a chosen interpolation algorithm.

**Key Parameters:**
- **input**: Tensor to resize (3D–5D: batch × channels × [depth] × [height] × width).
- **size** or **scale_factor**: Target output dimensions or scaling multiplier.
- **mode**: Interpolation method (`'nearest'`, `'bilinear'`, `'bicubic'`, `'trilinear'`, `'area'`, etc.).
- **align_corners**: Aligns corner pixels if `True` (affects linear, bilinear, bicubic, trilinear modes).
- **antialias**: Applies anti-aliasing when downsampling (supports `'bilinear'` and `'bicubic'`).

**Notes:**
- Use `clamp()` for `'bicubic'` mode to avoid overshoot (except `uint8` inputs).
- `'nearest-exact'` matches PIL/Scikit-Image; `'nearest'` matches OpenCV’s buggy behavior.
- CUDA `float16` gradients may be inaccurate for certain modes.
- Operation can be nondeterministic on CUDA devices.
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
