`torch.polar` constructs a complex tensor from polar coordinates, where `abs` provides the magnitude and `angle` gives the phase. The result is computed as:  
`abs * cos(angle) + abs * sin(angle) * j`.  

**Key points:**  
- Inputs `abs` and `angle` must be real tensors of matching dtype (float or double).  
- Output dtype is `torch.complex64` for float32 inputs, `torch.complex128` for float64.  
- It is **not** a polar decomposition; it simply converts polar to Cartesian complex form.  
- Undefined behavior if `abs` is negative/NaN or `angle` is infinite.  

**Example:**  
```python

angle = torch.tensor([np.pi/2, 5*np.pi/4], dtype=torch.float64)
z = torch.polar(abs, angle)  # tensor([(0+1j), (-1.4142-1.4142j)])
```
