`torch.cat` concatenates a sequence of tensors along a specified dimension. All tensors must have identical shapes except in the concatenation dimension, or be a 1‑D empty tensor of size `(0,)`. It serves as the inverse of operations like `torch.split()` and `torch.chunk()`.

**Parameters:**
- `tensors`: Sequence of tensors to concatenate.
- `dim` (optional): Dimension along which to concatenate (default is 0).
- `out` (optional): Output tensor.

**Example:**
```python
>>> x = torch.randn(2, 3)
>>> torch.cat((x, x, x), dim=0)  # Concatenate along rows → shape (6, 3)
>>> torch.cat((x, x, x), dim=1)  # Concatenate along columns → shape (2, 9)
```
