`torch.var_mean` computes both the variance and mean of a tensor along specified dimensions. It returns a tuple `(variance, mean)`.

**Key parameters:**
- `dim`: Dimension(s) to reduce; if `None`, computes over all elements.
- `correction`: Adjusts the denominator in variance calculation (default `1`, applying Bessel’s correction for sample variance).
- `keepdim`: If `True`, retains reduced dimensions as size 1.

**Formula:**  
Variance is calculated as  
σ² = Σ(xᵢ − x̄)² / max(0, N − correction)  
where N is the sample count and x̄ is the mean.

**Example:**
```python
var, mean = torch.var_mean(tensor, dim=0, keepdim=True)
```
