Instead of setting values to zero, it replaces them with the negative saturation value of the SELU activation function, preserving the self-normalizing properties of SELU networks. This helps maintain zero mean and unit variance in the activations.

It is particularly useful in convolutional layers where adjacent pixels are highly correlated, as it promotes independence between feature maps and provides better regularization than standard dropout in such cases.

- `p` (float, default 0.5): Probability of masking a channel.
- `inplace` (bool, default False): Whether to perform the operation in-place.

**Input/Output shape:** Supports both batched `(N, C, D, H, W)` and unbatched `(C, D, H, W)` tensors, returning the same shape as input.
