**FeatureAlphaDropout** is a PyTorch module that randomly masks entire channels (feature maps) in a tensor during training. Unlike standard dropout, it sets masked activations to the negative saturation value of the SELU activation function, preserving the self-normalizing properties of SELU networks.

**Key Features:**
- **Channel-wise dropout**: Masks entire channels instead of individual elements.
- **SELU-compatible**: Uses alpha dropout to maintain zero mean and unit variance, crucial for self-normalizing neural networks.
- **Use case**: Recommended for convolutional layers where adjacent pixels are highly correlated, as it promotes independence between feature maps.

**Parameters:**
- `p` (float, default=0.5): Probability of masking a channel.
- `inplace` (bool, default=False): If True, performs the operation in-place.

**Input/Output Shape:**  
Supports tensors of shape `(N, C, D, H, W)` or `(C, D, H, W)`. Output shape matches input.

**Example:**
```python
m = nn.FeatureAlphaDropout(p=0.2)
input = torch.randn(20, 16, 4, 32, 32)
output = m(input)
```
