**AlphaDropout** is a PyTorch dropout variant that preserves the **self-normalizing property** of inputs with zero mean and unit standard deviation. It is designed to work with the **SELU activation function**, ensuring outputs maintain the same mean and variance as inputs during training.

**Key Features:**
- **Maintains statistics**: Unlike standard dropout, it keeps the original mean (0) and standard deviation (1) of normalized inputs.
- **Training vs. evaluation**: Applies dropout only during training; acts as an identity function during evaluation.
- **Parameters**:  
  - `p` (float, default=0.5): Probability of dropping an element.  
  - `inplace` (bool, optional): If `True`, performs operation in-place.

**Usage Example:**
```python
m = nn.AlphaDropout(p=0.2)
input = torch.randn(20, 16)
output = m(input)  # During training: applies dropout; evaluation: passes through unchanged
```
