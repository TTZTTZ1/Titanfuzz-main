**AlphaDropout** is a dropout variant that preserves the self-normalizing property of inputs with zero mean and unit standard deviation. It is designed to work with SELU activation functions.

**Key Features:**
- Maintains original mean and standard deviation during training
- Randomly masks elements with probability `p` (default: 0.5)
- Scales and shifts masked elements to preserve normalization
- Acts as identity function during evaluation

**Parameters:**
- `p`: Dropout probability (float)
- `inplace`: Whether to perform operation in-place (bool, optional)

**Usage:**
```python

m = nn.AlphaDropout(p=0.2)
output = m(input_tensor)
```

**Shape:** Input and output have identical shapes.

**Purpose:** Primarily used with SELU activations to maintain self-normalizing network properties as described in the *Self-Normalizing Neural Networks* paper.
