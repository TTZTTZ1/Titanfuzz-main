`torch.svd_lowrank` computes a low‑rank singular value decomposition (SVD) of a matrix (or batch of matrices) \( A \approx U \operatorname{diag}(S) V^H \). It is a randomized algorithm suitable for large sparse matrices where a full SVD would be too expensive.

**Key parameters:**
- `A`: input tensor of shape `(*, m, n)`.
- `q`: slightly overestimated rank (default 6).
- `niter`: number of subspace iterations (default 2).
- `M`: optional mean tensor to subtract from `A` before decomposition.

**Notes:**
- Use when the matrix is too large for `torch.linalg.svd`, especially for sparse data.
- The rank parameter `q` should satisfy \( k \leq q \leq \min(2k, m, n) \), where \( k \) is the true rank.
- Results are non‑deterministic unless the random seed is fixed.
- Based on the randomized SVD algorithm by Halko et al. (2009).
