**Key parameters:**  
- `size`: Number of neighboring channels used for normalization.  
- `alpha`: Multiplicative factor (default: 0.0001).  
- `beta`: Exponent (default: 0.75).  
- `k`: Additive factor (default: 1).

\[
b_c = a_c \left( k + \frac{\alpha}{n} \sum_{c'} a_{c'}^2 \right)^{-\beta}
\]  
where the sum runs over \( n \) neighboring channels centered at \( c \).  

**Input/Output shape:**  
- Input: \((N, C, *)\)  
- Output: \((N, C, *)\) (same shape as input)  

**Example usage:**  
```python

output = lrn(input_tensor)  # input_tensor shape: (batch, channels, ...)
```  

This is commonly used in older CNN architectures (e.g., AlexNet) to encourage lateral inhibition between channels.
