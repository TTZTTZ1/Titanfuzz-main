`torch.linalg.qr` computes the QR decomposition of a matrix (or batch of matrices) into an orthogonal/unitary matrix **Q** and an upper-triangular matrix **R** such that **A = QR**.

**Key features:**
- Supports real and complex matrices, as well as batched inputs.
- Offers three modes via the `mode` parameter:
  - `'reduced'` (default): Returns **Q** (m × k) and **R** (k × n), where k = min(m, n). Always differentiable.
  - `'complete'`: Returns full **Q** (m × m) and **R** (m × n). Differentiable only when m ≤ n.
  - `'r'`: Returns only **R** (k × n), with **Q** as an empty tensor. Not differentiable.

**Important notes:**
- The diagonal of **R** is real but not guaranteed positive, so the decomposition is unique only up to the sign of the diagonal.
- For correct results, the first k columns of each input matrix must be linearly independent (otherwise, output may be invalid or autograd may fail).
- Unlike NumPy, `mode='raw'` is not supported, and a tuple `(Q, R)` is always returned (with **Q** empty for `mode='r'`).
