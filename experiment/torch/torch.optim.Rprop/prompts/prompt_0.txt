The **Rprop optimizer** in PyTorch implements the resilient backpropagation algorithm, which adapts step sizes individually for each parameter based on gradient sign changes.  

### **Key Features:**
- **Adaptive step sizes**:  
  - If the gradient sign remains the same (`g_prev * g_current > 0`), the step size is increased (multiplied by `etaplus`, up to `step_sizes[1]`).  
  - If the gradient sign changes (`g_prev * g_current < 0`), the step size is decreased (multiplied by `etaminus`, down to `step_sizes[0]`) and the current gradient is zeroed.  
  - Otherwise, the step size remains unchanged.  
- **Parameter update**:  
  Parameters are updated using:  
  `θ_t = θ_{t-1} - η_t * sign(g_t)`  
  where `sign(g_t)` is the sign of the gradient.  

### **Parameters:**
- `params` – Parameters to optimize.  
- `lr` (default: 0.01) – Initial learning rate (step size).  
- `etas` (default: (0.5, 1.2)) – Multiplicative factors for decreasing (`etaminus`) and increasing (`etaplus`) step sizes.  
- `step_sizes` (default: (1e-6, 50)) – Minimum and maximum allowed step sizes.  
- `capturable` (default: False) – Enables safe capture in CUDA graphs or `torch.compile`.  
- `foreach` (default: None) – Uses a faster foreach implementation on CUDA if possible (higher memory usage).  
- `maximize` (default: False) – Maximizes the objective instead of minimizing.  
- `differentiable` (default: False) – Allows autograd through the optimizer step (may impair performance).  

### **Common Methods:**
- `step(closure)` – Performs a single optimization step.  
- `zero_grad(set_to_none=True)` – Resets gradients (set to `None` to save memory).  
- `state_dict()` / `load_state_dict()` – Saves and loads optimizer state.  
- `add_param_group()` – Adds a new parameter group.  

### **Use Case:**
Rprop is suitable for full-batch optimization where stochastic gradients are not used, as it relies on gradient sign consistency across steps. It is not commonly used with mini-batch training due to its sensitivity to gradient noise.
