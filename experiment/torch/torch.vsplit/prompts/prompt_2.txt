`torch.vsplit` vertically splits a tensor (with two or more dimensions) into multiple tensors along the first dimension (dim=0). It is a convenience wrapper for `torch.tensor_split(input, indices_or_sections, dim=0)`.

**Key points:**
- **Input:** A tensor with at least two dimensions.
- **Splitting:**  
  - If `indices_or_sections` is an integer, the first dimension must be divisible by it.  
  - If it is a list or tuple of integers, splits occur at the specified indices along dim=0.
- **Output:** A list of tensors, each being a view of the original tensor.
- **Note:** Equivalent to NumPy's `numpy.vsplit()`.

**Example:**
```python
t = torch.arange(16.0).reshape(4,4)
torch.vsplit(t, 2)  # Splits into 2 equal parts vertically
```
