`torch.qr` computes the QR decomposition of a matrix (or batch of matrices) into an orthogonal matrix Q and an upper triangular matrix R, such that input = Q × R.  

**Key details:**  
- **Deprecated:** Use `torch.linalg.qr` instead.  
- **Parameters:**  
  - `input`: Tensor of shape `(*, m, n)`.  
  - `some` (bool):  
    - `True` → Thin/reduced QR (default, returns Q: m × k, R: k × n, where k = min(m, n)).  
    - `False` → Complete QR (returns Q: m × m, R: m × n).  
- **Note:** Backward pass is well-defined only if the first min(m, n) columns of input are linearly independent.  
- **Platforms:** Uses LAPACK (CPU) or MAGMA (CUDA), which may produce different valid results across devices.  

**Example replacement:**  
- `Q, R = torch.qr(A)` → `Q, R = torch.linalg.qr(A)`  
- `Q, R = torch.qr(A, some=False)` → `Q, R = torch.linalg.qr(A, mode="complete")`
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
