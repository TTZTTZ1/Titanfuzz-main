`torch.qr` computes the QR decomposition of a matrix (or batch of matrices) into an orthogonal matrix `Q` and an upper triangular matrix `R` such that `input = Q @ R`.  

**Key details:**
- **Deprecated:** Use `torch.linalg.qr` instead.
- **Parameter `some`:**  
  - `True` (default) → thin/reduced QR (smaller `Q`, `R`).  
  - `False` → complete QR (full `Q`, `R`).
- **Backpropagation warning:** Currently well-defined only when the first `min(m, n)` columns of `input` are linearly independent.
- **Platform/device differences:** Uses LAPACK (CPU) or MAGMA (CUDA), which may produce different valid decompositions.

**Example replacement:**
- `torch.qr(A)` → `torch.linalg.qr(A)`
- `torch.qr(A, some=False)` → `torch.linalg.qr(A, mode="complete")`
