The `MultiStepLR` learning rate scheduler decays the learning rate by a specified factor (`gamma`) at predefined epoch milestones.

**Key Features:**
- **Purpose:** Reduces learning rate at specific epochs to improve training convergence.
- **Parameters:**
  - `optimizer`: The optimizer whose learning rate will be adjusted.
  - `milestones`: A list of increasing epoch indices where the learning rate is multiplied by `gamma`.
  - `gamma`: Multiplicative factor for decay (default: 0.1).
  - `last_epoch`: The index of the last epoch (default: -1, starts from initial learning rate).

**Usage Example:**
```python
scheduler = MultiStepLR(optimizer, milestones=[30, 80], gamma=0.1)
for epoch in range(100):
    train(...)
    validate(...)
    scheduler.step()  # Call after optimizer.step()
```
- **Behavior:** Learning rate decays to 0.1× at epoch 30, and to 0.01× at epoch 80.

**Important Notes:**
- Milestones must be in increasing order.
- If an epoch appears multiple times in `milestones`, the learning rate is scaled by `gamma` raised to the power of the number of occurrences.
- Use `get_last_lr()` to retrieve the current learning rates.
- Call `scheduler.step()` after `optimizer.step()` during training.
