`torch.fft.fftshift` reorders FFT data to shift the zero-frequency component to the center of the tensor, arranging frequencies from negative to positive. It is commonly used to visualize FFT results or to convert between centered and uncentered grid conventions.

**Key points:**
- Moves the origin `(0, ..., 0)` to the center of the tensor along specified dimensions.
- By default, it operates on all dimensions.
- For even-sized dimensions, the Nyquist frequency is placed at index 0 after shifting.
- Often paired with `ifftshift` for switching between centered and uncentered grid representations.

**Example:**
```python
f = torch.fft.fftfreq(4)  # [0.0, 0.25, -0.5, -0.25]
torch.fft.fftshift(f)     # [-0.5, -0.25, 0.0, 0.25]
```
