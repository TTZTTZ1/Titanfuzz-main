`torch.roll` cyclically shifts elements of a tensor along specified dimensions. Elements shifted past the boundary wrap around to the opposite end.

**Key points:**
- **Parameters:**  
  - `input`: The tensor to shift.  
  - `shifts`: Number of positions to shift (can be a tuple for multiple dimensions).  
  - `dims`: Dimension(s) along which to roll (if `None`, flattens the tensor first).  
- **Behavior:**  
  - Positive `shifts` move elements forward; negative moves them backward.  
  - If `shifts` and `dims` are tuples, each dimension is shifted by its corresponding value.  
- **Example:**  
  Rolling a 4×2 matrix by `(2, 1)` in dimensions `(0, 1)` shifts rows down by 2 and columns right by 1, with wrap-around.
