`torch.roll` cyclically shifts elements of a tensor along specified dimensions. Elements shifted past the end wrap around to the beginning.

**Key behavior:**
- `shifts`: Number of positions to shift (positive or negative).
- `dims`: Dimension(s) to shift along. If `None`, flattens the tensor, shifts, then restores shape.
- Supports single or multiple dimensions via tuples for `shifts` and `dims`.

**Example highlights:**
- Roll entire flattened tensor: `torch.roll(x, 1)`
- Roll along a specific dimension: `torch.roll(x, 1, 0)`
- Roll multiple dimensions simultaneously: `torch.roll(x, shifts=(2,1), dims=(0,1))`
