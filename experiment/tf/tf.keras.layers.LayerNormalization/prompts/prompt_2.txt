---GENERAL SUMMARY START---
The `tf.keras.layers.LayerNormalization` layer normalizes activations per data sample (independently across the batch), unlike batch normalization which normalizes across the batch. It stabilizes training by maintaining mean activation near 0 and standard deviation near 1 for each example.

**Key features:**
- **Normalization axis:** Typically the feature axis (default `axis=-1`), while batch dimensions are excluded.
- **Learnable parameters:** Optionally scales by `gamma` and shifts by `beta` (enabled by default).
- **Equations:** For each sample, it computes mean and variance, normalizes, then applies scaling and shifting.
- **RMS scaling option:** An approximate faster variant (`rms_scaling=True`) that avoids computing the mean.

**Common use cases:** Transformers, attention mechanisms, and recurrent networks where batch statistics are unstable or batch size is small.

**Main arguments:**
- `axis`: Axis/axes to normalize (default `-1`).
- `epsilon`: Small constant for numerical stability (default `0.001`).
- `center`/`scale`: Enable beta (shift) and gamma (scale) (default `True`).
- `rms_scaling`: Use faster RMS-based normalization instead of mean/variance.
- Initializers, regularizers, and constraints for `beta` and `gamma`.

**Example:**
```python
layer = LayerNormalization(axis=[1, 2, 3])
layer.build([5, 20, 30, 40])  # gamma/beta shape: (20, 30, 40)
```
