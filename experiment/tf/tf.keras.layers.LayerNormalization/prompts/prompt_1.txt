---GENERAL SUMMARY START---
`tf.keras.layers.LayerNormalization` is a normalization layer that normalizes activations **per data sample** (independently across the batch), unlike batch normalization which normalizes across the batch. It stabilizes training by maintaining mean activations near 0 and standard deviation near 1 for each example.

**Key Parameters:**
- `axis`: Axis/axes to normalize (default `-1`, the last dimension).
- `epsilon`: Small constant to avoid division by zero (default 0.001).
- `center`/`scale`: Whether to use trainable offset (`beta`) and scale (`gamma`) parameters (both default to `True`).
- `rms_scaling`: If `True`, uses a faster approximation ignoring mean computation.

**How it works:**
For each sample, it computes:
1. Mean and variance across the specified `axis`.
2. Normalizes using:  
   `(input - mean) / sqrt(variance + epsilon)`
3. Applies learned scaling (`gamma`) and shifting (`beta`) if enabled.

**Use cases:**  
Commonly applied in Transformer architectures, recurrent networks, and scenarios where batch statistics are unreliable (e.g., small batch sizes or variable-length sequences).
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
