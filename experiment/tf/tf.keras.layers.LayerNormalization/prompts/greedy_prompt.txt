# First, here is a general summary of the API's functionality and usage:
---GENERAL SUMMARY START---
`tf.keras.layers.LayerNormalization` is a layer that normalizes activations **independently for each sample** in a batch (unlike batch normalization, which normalizes across the batch). It stabilizes training by maintaining per-sample mean activations near 0 and standard deviation near 1.

**Key parameters:**
- `axis`: Axis/axes to normalize (default: `-1`, the last dimension).
- `epsilon`: Small constant to avoid division by zero (default: 0.001).
- `center`/`scale`: If `True`, learnable offset (`beta`) and scale (`gamma`) parameters are applied.
- `rms_scaling`: If `True`, uses a faster approximation ignoring mean computation.

**How it works:**
For each sample, it computes:
1. Mean and variance across the specified `axis`.
2. Normalizes: `(input - mean) / sqrt(variance + epsilon)`.
3. Optionally scales by `gamma` and shifts by `beta` (trainable).

**Use cases:**  
Commonly applied in transformers, recurrent networks, and other architectures where batch statistics are unreliable or batch size varies.
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
