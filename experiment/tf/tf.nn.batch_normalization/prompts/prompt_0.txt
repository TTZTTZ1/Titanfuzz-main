\[

- \(x\) is the input tensor  
- \(\mu\) is the mean  
- \(\sigma\) is the standard deviation (computed as \(\sqrt{\text{variance} + \epsilon}\))

- \(\epsilon\) (`variance_epsilon`) is a small constant to avoid division by zero  

**Key points:**  
- `mean`, `variance`, `offset`, and `scale` can either match the input shape (with dimensions normalized over set to 1) or be 1D tensors aligned with the depth dimension.  
- Typically used during inference with precomputed moving averages of mean and variance from training.  
- Helps stabilize and accelerate deep network training by reducing internal covariate shift.  

**Args:**  
- `x`: Input tensor  
- `mean`: Mean tensor  
- `variance`: Variance tensor  
- `offset`: Optional offset tensor (\(\beta\))  
- `scale`: Optional scale tensor (\(\gamma\))  
- `variance_epsilon`: Small float to prevent division by zero  
- `name`: Optional operation name  

**Returns:**  
Normalized, scaled, and offset tensor.
