`tf.nn.batch_normalization` performs batch normalization on an input tensor using the formula:  
\[
\frac{\gamma(x - \mu)}{\sigma} + \beta
\]  
where \(\mu\) is the mean, \(\sigma\) is the standard deviation (derived from variance), \(\gamma\) is an optional scale, and \(\beta\) is an optional offset.

**Key points:**
- **Inputs**:  
  - `x`: Input tensor of any shape.  
  - `mean` and `variance`: Tensors matching the normalized dimensions of `x` (can be 1D for the depth dimension or full shape with `keepdims=True`).  
  - `offset` (\(\beta\)) and `scale` (\(\gamma\)): Optional tensors applied after normalization.  
  - `variance_epsilon`: Small constant to prevent division by zero.  

- **Usage**:  
  Commonly used with `tf.nn.moments` to compute mean and variance during training. Supports both training (using batch statistics) and inference (using running averages).  

- **Purpose**:  
  Stabilizes and accelerates deep network training by reducing internal covariate shift, as introduced in the 2015 paper by Ioffe and Szegedy.
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
