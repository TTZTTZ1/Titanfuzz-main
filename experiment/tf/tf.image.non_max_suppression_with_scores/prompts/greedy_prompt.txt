`tf.image.non_max_suppression_with_scores` performs greedy selection of bounding boxes based on scores, using either standard Non-Max Suppression (NMS) or Soft-NMS. It prunes boxes with high overlap, returning both the indices of selected boxes and their updated scores.

**Key Features:**
- **Input:** Boxes (`[num_boxes, 4]` as `[y1, x1, y2, x2]`) and scores (`[num_boxes]`).
- **Output:** Selected indices and their corresponding scores (scores may be modified in Soft-NMS mode).
- **Modes:** 
  - Standard NMS: Uses `iou_threshold` to suppress overlapping boxes (`soft_nms_sigma=0.0`, default).
  - Soft-NMS: Applies Gaussian weighting to reduce scores of overlapping boxes instead of pruning them directly (`soft_nms_sigma > 0`). In this mode, `iou_threshold` is ignored.
- **Parameters:** 
  - `max_output_size`: Maximum number of boxes to select.
  - `score_threshold`: Minimum score for box consideration.
  - `soft_nms_sigma`: Sigma parameter controlling Soft-NMS behavior; set to `0.0` for standard NMS.

**Usage Example:**
```python
selected_indices, selected_scores = tf.image.non_max_suppression_with_scores(
    boxes, scores, max_output_size, iou_threshold=0.5, soft_nms_sigma=0.5)
selected_boxes = tf.gather(boxes, selected_indices)
```

This function extends `tf.image.non_max_suppression` by supporting Soft-NMS and returning updated scores, providing more flexibility in handling overlapping detections.
