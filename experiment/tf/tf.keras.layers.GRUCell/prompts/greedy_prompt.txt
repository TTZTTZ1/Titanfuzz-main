`tf.keras.layers.GRUCell` is a single-step recurrent cell implementing the Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) architecture. It processes one timestep at a time, making it suitable for use within `keras.layers.RNN` for sequential data.

**Key Features:**
- **Purpose**: Handles one timestep of input, unlike `keras.layers.GRU` which processes entire sequences.
- **Core Parameters**: 
  - `units`: Dimensionality of the output space.
  - `activation`/`recurrent_activation`: Activation functions (default: `tanh` and `sigmoid`).
  - `dropout`/`recurrent_dropout`: Optional dropout for inputs and recurrent states.
  - `reset_after`: Controls reset gate placement (default `True` for cuDNN compatibility).
- **Call Arguments**: Accepts `inputs` (batch, features) and `states` (previous hidden state).
- **Use Case**: Wrapped by `keras.layers.RNN` to build custom recurrent networks with options like `return_sequences` or `return_state`.

**Example Usage:**
```python
cell = keras.layers.GRUCell(units=4)
rnn = keras.layers.RNN(cell, return_sequences=True)
output = rnn(input_sequence)  # Processes full sequence
```

This cell is ideal for custom RNN implementations where fine-grained control over timestep processing is needed.
