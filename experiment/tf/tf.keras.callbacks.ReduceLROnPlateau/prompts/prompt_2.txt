`ReduceLROnPlateau` is a Keras callback that automatically reduces the learning rate when a monitored metric (e.g., validation loss) stops improving. It helps optimize training by adjusting the learning rate during stagnation, often leading to better model convergence.

**Key Parameters:**
- `monitor`: Metric to watch (default: `'val_loss'`).
- `factor`: Multiplier for reducing learning rate (e.g., `0.1`).
- `patience`: Epochs to wait before reducing LR after improvement stops.
- `mode`: Direction of improvement (`'min'`, `'max'`, or `'auto'`).
- `min_lr`: Minimum learning rate allowed.

**How it works:**  
If no improvement is seen for `patience` epochs, the learning rate is multiplied by `factor`. A `cooldown` period can optionally delay further adjustments after a reduction.

**Example usage:**
```python
reduce_lr = ReduceLROnPlateau(monitor='val_loss', factor=0.5, patience=5)
model.fit(x_train, y_train, callbacks=[reduce_lr])
```
