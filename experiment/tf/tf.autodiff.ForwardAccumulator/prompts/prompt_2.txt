### Key Features:
- **Purpose**: Computes JVPs by multiplying the Jacobian matrix of a function with a specified tangent vector.
- **Use Case**: Ideal for scenarios where you need gradients of many outputs with respect to few inputs (e.g., computing Hessian-vector products).
- **Comparison**: Unlike `tf.GradientTape` (which computes vector-Jacobian products via backprop), forward-mode processes one column of the Jacobian at a time, while backprop processes one row.

### Nesting Behavior:
- Accumulators must be strictly nested; inner accumulators do not see JVPs from outer ones.
- Can be combined with `tf.GradientTape` for efficient higher-order differentiation (e.g., Hessian-vector products).

### Arguments:

- `tangents`: Tensor/structure with same nesting as `primals`, defining the JVP direction.
