`tf.keras.layers.RandomZoom` is a preprocessing layer that randomly zooms images during training, either zooming in or out on each axis independently. It handles images of any pixel range or dtype, outputting floats by default.

**Key Parameters:**
- `height_factor`: Range for vertical zoom (positive = zoom out, negative = zoom in).
- `width_factor`: Optional range for horizontal zoom; if `None`, aspect ratio is preserved.
- `fill_mode`: How to fill empty space (`"constant"`, `"nearest"`, `"wrap"`, or `"reflect"`).
- `interpolation`: Method for resizing (`"nearest"` or `"bilinear"`).
- `seed`: For reproducible randomness.
- `data_format`: `"channels_last"` (default) or `"channels_first"`.

**Usage Example:**
```python
layer = keras.layers.RandomZoom(height_factor=0.5, width_factor=0.2)
```
