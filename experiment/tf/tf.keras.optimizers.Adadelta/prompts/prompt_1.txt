The `tf.keras.optimizers.Adadelta` optimizer implements the Adadelta algorithm, a stochastic gradient descent method designed to address issues like decaying learning rates and reliance on a manually set global learning rate. It adapts learning rates per dimension using a moving window of gradient updates rather than accumulating all past gradients, enabling continued learning even after many updates.

**Key Features:**
- **Adaptive Learning Rates:** Automatically adjusts learning rates for each parameter.
- **Robustness:** More stable than Adagrad, as it avoids aggressive decay of learning rates.
- **Flexible Configuration:** Supports various hyperparameters and advanced options like gradient clipping, weight decay, and exponential moving averages (EMA).

**Main Parameters:**
- `learning_rate`: Initial learning rate (default 0.001; higher values like 1.0 may be beneficial).
- `rho`: Decay rate for gradient moving average (default 0.95).
- `epsilon`: Small constant for numerical stability (default 1e-07).
- Optional features: `weight_decay`, gradient clipping (`clipnorm`, `clipvalue`), EMA (`use_ema`), loss scaling, and gradient accumulation.

**Typical Use Case:**
Adadelta is suitable for training deep neural networks where adaptive learning rates can improve convergence, especially in scenarios with sparse gradients or non-stationary objectives.

**Reference:** Based on the paper by Zeiler (2012).
