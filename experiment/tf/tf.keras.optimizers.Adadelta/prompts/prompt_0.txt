- **Adaptive Learning Rates:** Adjusts learning rates based on recent gradients, avoiding continuous decay.

**Main Parameters:**

- `epsilon`: Small value for numerical stability (default 1e-07).
- Optional features: Weight decay, gradient clipping, exponential moving average (EMA), loss scaling, and gradient accumulation.

**Typical Use Case:**  
Suitable for training deep neural networks, especially when dealing with sparse gradients or non-stationary objectives, as it automatically adapts learning rates without manual tuning.

**Reference:**  
Based on the paper by Zeiler (2012), which introduces Adadelta as an extension of Adagrad.
