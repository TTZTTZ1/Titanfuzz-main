`tf.keras.initializers.glorot_normal` (also called Xavier normal initializer) is a weight initializer that draws values from a truncated normal distribution with a standard deviation of `sqrt(2 / (fan_in + fan_out))`, where `fan_in` and `fan_out` are the input and output units of the weight tensor. This helps maintain stable variance across layers during neural network training.

**Key Features:**
- **Purpose:** Initializes weights to promote efficient gradient flow in deep networks.
- **Distribution:** Truncated normal distribution centered at 0.
- **Usage:** Can be used standalone or as a `kernel_initializer` in Keras layers (e.g., `Dense`, `Conv2D`).
- **Seed Control:** Accepts a seed for deterministic initialization.

**Example:**
```python
initializer = tf.keras.initializers.glorot_normal(seed=42)
layer = tf.keras.layers.Dense(64, kernel_initializer=initializer)
```
