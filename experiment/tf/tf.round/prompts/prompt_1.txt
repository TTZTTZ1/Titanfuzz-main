`tf.round` (or `tf.math.round`) performs element-wise rounding of a tensor's values to the nearest integer, using **"half to even" (bankers' rounding)**.  

**Key points:**  
- **Input:** A tensor of type `float16`, `float32`, `float64`, `int32`, or `int64`.  
- **Output:** A tensor of the same shape and type as the input.  
- **Rounding rule:** Ties (values exactly halfway between integers) are rounded to the nearest even integer.  
- **Example:**  
  ```python
  tf.round([0.9, 2.5, 2.3, 1.5, -4.5])  # [1.0, 2.0, 2.0, 2.0, -4.0]
  ```  
- **Note:** For system-dependent rounding, use `tf.cast` with `tf.int` types instead.
