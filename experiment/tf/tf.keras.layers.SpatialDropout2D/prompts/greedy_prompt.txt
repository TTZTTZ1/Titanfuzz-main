It is particularly useful in convolutional neural networks (CNNs) where adjacent pixels in feature maps are highly correlated, as standard dropout may be ineffective. By dropping whole feature maps, it encourages independence between them and helps prevent overfitting.

- `rate`: Fraction of input units to drop (between 0 and 1).
- `data_format`: Specifies channel order (`"channels_first"` or `"channels_last"`). Defaults to the Keras configuration.

**Usage:**
- **Input:** A 4D tensor with shape depending on `data_format`.
- **Output:** Same shape as input.
- **Behavior:** Dropout is applied only during training (`training=True`).

**Example:**
```python
layer = SpatialDropout2D(rate=0.5, data_format='channels_last')
```
