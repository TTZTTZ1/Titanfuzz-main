The `tf.keras.optimizers.Adam` optimizer implements the Adam algorithm, a widely used stochastic gradient descent method that adaptively estimates first-order (momentum) and second-order moments for efficient optimization. It is suitable for large-scale problems due to its computational efficiency and low memory requirements.

**Key Features:**
- **Adaptive Learning:** Adjusts learning rates based on estimates of gradient moments.
- **Hyperparameters:** Includes `beta_1` (momentum decay), `beta_2` (second-moment decay), `epsilon` (numerical stability), and optional AMSGrad variant.
- **Advanced Options:** Supports weight decay, gradient clipping, exponential moving averages (EMA), loss scaling for mixed precision, and gradient accumulation.

**Common Usage:**
```python
optimizer = tf.keras.optimizers.Adam(
    learning_rate=0.001,
    beta_1=0.9,
    beta_2=0.999,
    epsilon=1e-07
)
model.compile(optimizer=optimizer, ...)
```

**Use Cases:** Training deep learning models across various tasks (e.g., regression, GANs, distributed training), especially when dealing with noisy gradients or large datasets.
