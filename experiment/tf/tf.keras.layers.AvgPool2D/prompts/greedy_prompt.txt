`tf.keras.layers.AvgPool2D` (also called `AveragePooling2D`) performs **2D average pooling** on spatial data (height and width). It downsamples input by computing the **average value** within a sliding window of size `pool_size`, moving according to `strides`.

**Key Parameters:**
- `pool_size`: Size of the pooling window (int or tuple of 2 ints).
- `strides`: Step size for sliding the window (defaults to `pool_size` if not set).
- `padding`: `"valid"` (no padding) or `"same"` (pads to preserve spatial dimensions).
- `data_format`: `"channels_last"` (default, shape: `[batch, height, width, channels]`) or `"channels_first"` (shape: `[batch, channels, height, width]`).

**Output Shape:**

**Example:**
```python

```
This layer is commonly used in convolutional neural networks to reduce feature map size and computational load.
