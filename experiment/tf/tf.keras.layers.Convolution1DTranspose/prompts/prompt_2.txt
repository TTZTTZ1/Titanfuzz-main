`tf.keras.layers.Conv1DTranspose` (also called `Convolution1DTranspose`) performs a 1D transposed convolution operation, which is commonly used for upsampling or generating outputs with larger spatial dimensions than the input. - `kernel_size`: Size of the convolution window.
- `strides`: Stride length; must be 1 if `dilation_rate` > 1.

- `data_format`: `"channels_last"` (default, shape `[batch, steps, channels]`) or `"channels_first"` (shape `[batch, channels, steps]`).

**Input/Output Shapes:**
- **Input:** 3D tensor `(batch, steps, channels)` (channels_last) or `(batch, channels, steps)` (channels_first).
- **Output:** 3D tensor with `steps` dimension transformed based on `kernel_size`, `strides`, and `padding`.

**Example:**
```python
x = np.random.rand(4, 10, 128)
y = keras.layers.Conv1DTranspose(32, 3, strides=2, activation='relu')(x)
# Output shape: (4, 21, 32)
```

**Common Use Cases:** Upsampling in audio processing, time-series generation, and decoder networks in autoencoders or GANs.
