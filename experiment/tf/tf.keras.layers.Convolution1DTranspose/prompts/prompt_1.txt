`tf.keras.layers.Conv1DTranspose` (also called `Convolution1DTranspose`) is a 1D transposed convolution layer used for upsampling or generating outputs with larger spatial dimensions than the input. It reverses the spatial transformation of a regular convolution while maintaining the connectivity pattern.

**Key Features:**
- **Purpose:** Increases the length (`steps`) dimension of 1D sequences (e.g., time series, audio signals).
- **Parameters:**  
  - `filters`: Number of output channels.  
  - `kernel_size`: Size of the convolution window.  
  - `strides`: Upsampling factor (default 1).  
  - `padding`: `"valid"` (no padding) or `"same"` (output length matches input length when `strides=1`).  
  - `data_format`: `"channels_last"` (default, shape `[batch, steps, channels]`) or `"channels_first"` (`[batch, channels, steps]`).  
  - `activation`, `use_bias`, initializers, regularizers, and constraints are supported.

**Input/Output Shapes:**
- **Input:** 3D tensor `(batch, steps, channels)` (channels_last) or `(batch, channels, steps)` (channels_first).
- **Output:** 3D tensor with upsampled `steps` dimension and `filters` channels.

**Example:**
```python
x = np.random.rand(4, 10, 128)  # (batch, steps, channels)
y = Conv1DTranspose(filters=32, kernel_size=3, strides=2, activation='relu')(x)

```

**Note:** `strides > 1` and `dilation_rate > 1` cannot be used simultaneously.
