The `tf.keras.layers.Dropout` layer applies **random dropout** during training to help prevent overfitting by setting a fraction of input units to 0 at each training step. The remaining inputs are scaled by `1/(1 - rate)` to preserve the overall sum.

**Key parameters:**
- `rate`: Fraction of inputs to drop (between 0 and 1).
- `noise_shape`: Optional shape for the dropout mask (e.g., to apply the same mask across certain dimensions).
- `seed`: Optional random seed for reproducibility.

**Important behavior:**
- Dropout is **only active when `training=True`** (e.g., automatically set in `model.fit`).
- During inference (`training=False`), dropout is disabled and the layer passes inputs unchanged.
- Unlike trainable layers, dropout has no learnable weights, so setting `trainable=False` does not affect its behavior.

**Common use:** Added to neural networks as a regularization technique during training.
