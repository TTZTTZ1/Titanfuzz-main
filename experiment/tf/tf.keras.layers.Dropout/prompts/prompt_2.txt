---GENERAL SUMMARY START---
The `tf.keras.layers.Dropout` layer is a regularization technique used during training to prevent overfitting by randomly setting a fraction (`rate`) of input units to 0 at each training step. The remaining inputs are scaled by `1/(1 - rate)` to maintain the overall sum.

**Key Points:**
- **Purpose:** Reduces overfitting in neural networks.
- **Behavior:** Only active when `training=True` (automatically set in `model.fit`).
- **Parameters:**
  - `rate`: Fraction of inputs to drop (0 to 1).
  - `noise_shape`: Optional shape for custom dropout masks (e.g., same mask across timesteps).
  - `seed`: For reproducible randomness.
- **Usage:** Applied during training; inactive during inference/prediction.
- **Note:** Unlike other layers, `trainable` has no effect on Dropout.

**Example:**
```python
tf.keras.layers.Dropout(rate=0.5)  # Drops 50% of inputs during training
```
