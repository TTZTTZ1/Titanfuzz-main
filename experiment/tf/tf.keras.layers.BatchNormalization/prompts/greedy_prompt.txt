**BatchNormalization** is a layer that normalizes its inputs to stabilize and accelerate neural network training. It works differently during training and inference:

- **During training**: Normalizes each batch using its own mean and variance, applying learned scaling (`gamma`) and offset (`beta`) parameters.
- **During inference**: Uses moving averages of mean and variance accumulated during training.

**Key parameters:**
- `axis`: Axis to normalize (e.g., features axis).
- `momentum`: Controls updating of moving statistics.
- `epsilon`: Small constant to avoid division by zero.
- `center`/`scale`: Enable/disable beta (offset) and gamma (scaling).
- `training`: Boolean to switch between training/inference modes.

**Important behavior:**
- Setting `layer.trainable = False` forces inference mode (uses moving statistics).
- Supports regularization and constraints on gamma/beta.
- For distributed training, `synchronized=True` synchronizes batch statistics across devices.

**Use cases:** Improves convergence, reduces internal covariate shift, and acts as a regularizer. Commonly used after convolutional or dense layers.
---GENERAL SUMMARY END---
