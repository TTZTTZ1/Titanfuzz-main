The `tf.keras.metrics.KLDivergence` metric computes the Kullback–Leibler divergence between the true probability distribution (`y_true`) and the predicted distribution (`y_pred`). It is used to measure how one probability distribution differs from another.

**Key points:**
- **Formula:** `y_true * log(y_true / y_pred)`
- **Inputs:** Both `y_true` and `y_pred` should be probability distributions with values between 0 and 1 (values are clipped to [0, 1]).
- **Usage:** Commonly used in probabilistic models to evaluate how well the predicted distribution matches the true distribution.
- **Integration:** Can be added as a metric in `model.compile()` to monitor during training.
