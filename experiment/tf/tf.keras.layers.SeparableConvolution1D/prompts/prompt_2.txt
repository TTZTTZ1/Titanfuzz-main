The `tf.keras.layers.SeparableConv1D` layer performs a 1D separable convolution, which consists of two steps:  
- A **depthwise convolution** applied independently to each input channel.  
- A **pointwise convolution** (1×1 convolution) that mixes the channels to produce the final output.  

This approach reduces computational cost compared to a standard convolution while maintaining effectiveness, especially for depthwise separable operations.

### **Key Parameters**
- **`filters`**: Number of output filters (dimensionality of the output space).  
- **`kernel_size`**: Size of the depthwise convolution window.  
- **`strides`**: Stride length for the convolution.  
- **`padding`**: `"valid"` (no padding) or `"same"` (padding to preserve input length when `strides=1`).  
- **`data_format`**: `"channels_last"` (default, shape: `(batch, steps, channels)`) or `"channels_first"` (shape: `(batch, channels, steps)`).  
- **`depth_multiplier`**: Number of depthwise output channels per input channel.  
- **`activation`**: Activation function applied to the output (e.g., `'relu'`).  

### **Input & Output Shapes**
- **Input**: 3D tensor with shape depending on `data_format`.  
- **Output**: 3D tensor with the same format, where the channel dimension becomes `filters` and spatial dimension (`steps`) may change based on `padding` and `strides`.  

### **Example**
```python
x = np.random.rand(4, 10, 12)  # 4 samples, 10 time steps, 12 channels

```

### **Use Case**
Ideal for lightweight 1D signal or sequence processing (e.g., audio, time-series), where reducing parameters and computations is beneficial without sacrificing representational capacity.
