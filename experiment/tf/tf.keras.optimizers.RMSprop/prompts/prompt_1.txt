---GENERAL SUMMARY START---
The `tf.keras.optimizers.RMSprop` optimizer implements the RMSprop algorithm, which adapts learning rates by maintaining a moving average of squared gradients and dividing gradients by the root of this average. It is commonly used for training neural networks, particularly in recurrent networks and non-stationary problems.

**Key Features:**
- **Adaptive Learning:** Adjusts learning rates per parameter using a decaying average of past squared gradients.
- **Momentum Support:** Optional plain momentum (not Nesterov) to accelerate convergence.
- **Centered Variant:** An optional centered version normalizes gradients by estimated variance for potentially better training stability.
- **Additional Optimizations:** Supports weight decay, gradient clipping, exponential moving averages (EMA), loss scaling, and gradient accumulation.

**Main Parameters:**
- `learning_rate`: Base learning rate (default 0.001).
- `rho`: Discount factor for gradient history (default 0.9).
- `momentum`: Momentum coefficient (default 0.0).
- `epsilon`: Small constant for numerical stability (default 1e-7).
- `centered`: Whether to use centered RMSprop (default False).

**Typical Usage:**
```python
opt = keras.optimizers.RMSprop(learning_rate=0.01)
model.compile(optimizer=opt, loss='mse')
```

RMSprop is effective for problems with noisy or sparse gradients and is a popular choice in various deep learning applications.
