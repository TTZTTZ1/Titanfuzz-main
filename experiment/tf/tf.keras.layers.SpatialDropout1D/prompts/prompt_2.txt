`tf.keras.layers.SpatialDropout1D` is a regularization layer that drops entire 1D feature maps (channels) during training, rather than individual elements. It is particularly useful for sequential data (e.g., time series, audio) where adjacent time steps are highly correlated, preventing standard dropout from effectively regularizing activations.

**Key Features:**
- **Purpose:** Promotes independence between feature maps in early convolutional layers.
- **Input:** 3D tensor with shape `(samples, timesteps, channels)`.
- **Output:** Same shape as input.
- **Arguments:**
  - `rate`: Fraction of input channels to drop (float between 0 and 1).
  - `training`: Boolean to toggle dropout behavior (applied only during training).

**Usage Example:**
```python
layer = tf.keras.layers.SpatialDropout1D(rate=0.5)
output = layer(inputs, training=True)  # Drops random channels during training
```

**Reference:** Based on research by Tompson et al. (2014) for effective regularization in convolutional networks.
