It is particularly useful for sequential data (like time series or audio) where adjacent timesteps are highly correlated, as it helps promote independence between channels and prevents ineffective regularization that standard dropout might cause in such cases.

- **Purpose:** Regularizes by dropping entire channels in 3D input data.
- **Input Shape:** 3D tensor `(samples, timesteps, channels)`.
- **Output Shape:** Same as input.
- **Arguments:**

  - `training`: Boolean to toggle dropout behavior during training vs. inference.
- **Use Case:** Preferred over standard dropout in early convolutional layers for sequential data to avoid merely reducing the learning rate.
